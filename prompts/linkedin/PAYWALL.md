# Paywall Playbook

Use this guide when the post is a Sauce post about paywalls, trials, subscription pricing, offer logic, or monetization conversion.

Operating contract:
- Treat this as execution guidance, not as universal truth.
- Keep advice specific to funnel stage and traffic quality.
- Do not copy lines from this file into final output.
- Use factual language carefully, and avoid unsupported benchmarks.

Practical structure:
1. Open with a high-friction monetization reality teams ignore.
2. Diagnose where the funnel is leaking: placement, offer, clarity, or traffic fit.
3. Explain why the leak happens in behavior terms.
4. Give one to three concrete actions teams can ship quickly.
5. Add caveats for audience intent, geo mix, and pricing context.

Optimization order:
- Ensure paywall coverage exists at high-intent moments first.
- Fix sequencing and offer logic before polishing micro-copy.
- Improve value clarity and risk reduction before discount escalation.
- Test pricing changes after structure and message quality are stable.

Signals and mechanics:
- High trial starts with weak trial-to-paid usually indicates fit or onboarding value gaps.
- Low trial starts with decent trial-to-paid often indicates offer clarity or paywall friction.
- Good conversion with weak retention can indicate acquisition mismatch.
- Weak monetization with strong top-of-funnel can indicate packaging and timing issues.

Tactical patterns to surface:
- Clear value proposition beats clever wording.
- Step-by-step paywalls often win when each step has one job.
- One dominant CTA usually outperforms cluttered competing actions.
- Price anchoring should reduce cognitive load, not increase it.
- Fallback offers should feel contextual and earned, not random discount spam.

Sauce quality bar:
- Include at least one concrete metric or diagnostic threshold.
- Include one explicit caveat such as channel mix, cohort quality, or app category.
- Close with a practical test idea that teams can run this week.
