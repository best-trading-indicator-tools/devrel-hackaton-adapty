### #1 LTV
Weekly + trial shows the strongest 12-month LTV signal among common configurations.

**CONTEXT**
Most app teams treat weekly subscriptions as a low-value acquisition play — a cheap entry point that churns fast. But LTV curves tell a radically different story. Weekly trial cohorts start at a modest $7.13 on Day 0, but compound aggressively over 380 days to reach $54.17 — a 660% growth rate. No other cohort comes close. If you're not actively optimizing your weekly trial funnel, you're leaving one of your highest-LTV growth levers completely untouched.

**DATA EVIDENCE**
| Configuration | D0 LTV | D380 LTV | 12-Month Growth |
| Weekly + Trial | $7.40 | $54.50 | +636.5% |
| Annual + Trial | $42.08 | $49.92 | +18.6% |
| Monthly + Trial | $10.32 | $35.28 | +241.9% |
| Weekly + Direct | $6.45 | $26.98 | +318.3% |
| Annual + Direct | $35.79 | $42.45 | +18.6% |
| Monthly + Direct | $10.65 | $29.73 | +179.2% |

**ACTIONABLE TAKEAWAYS**
1. Invest in weekly trial UX: the first 30 days of a weekly trial cohort are when LTV compounds fastest (from $7.13 to $18.72 by day 33 — a 163% jump in one month).
2. A/B test your weekly plan pricing. If you're under $7/week, you may be leaving money on the table, given this cohort's long-term retention signal.
3. Rethink how you report weekly plan performance. Don't evaluate at Day 0 or even Day 30. The real signal emerges at Day 99+.
4. Build re-engagement campaigns specifically for weekly trial subscribers at the 30–60 day window to capture the compounding curve.

### #2 LTV
Trials don't work the same everywhere. In half of the app categories, direct buyers end up paying you more than trial users.

**CONTEXT**
The conventional wisdom is "offer a free trial, and you will get better-quality subscribers." The global average backs this up: trial cohorts on annual plans generate $49.92 vs $42.45 for direct buyers — an 18% premium. But this average conceals a critical split. In Utilities, the trial premium is +85.1%. In Health & Fitness, it is +63.6%. In Education, it is +50.4%. However, in Productivity, Lifestyle, Entertainment, and Graphics & Design, trial users are worth less than direct buyers — sometimes significantly so (Lifestyle: -21.2%, Productivity: -13.7%). Categories where the product value is immediately tangible benefit the most from trial conversion. Categories where engagement is self-driven suffer from trial churn.

**DATA EVIDENCE**
| Category | Annual direct LTV D380 | Annual trial LTV D380 | Trial Premium |
| Utilities | $37.23 | $68.90 | +85.1% |
| Health & Fitness | $31.71 | $51.86 | +63.6% |
| Education | $42.11 | $63.34 | +50.4% |
| Photo & Video | $39.73 | $46.87 | +17.9% |
| Graphics & Design | $43.24 | $41.48 | -4.1% |
| Entertainment | $52.50 | $49.81 | -5.1% |
| Lifestyle | $45.68 | $36.01 | -21.2% |
| Productivity | $56.95 | $49.13 | -13.7% |

Global: trial $49.92 vs direct $42.45 (18% premium).

**ACTIONABLE TAKEAWAYS**
- If you are in Utilities, Health & Fitness, or Education, aggressively promote free trials.
- If you are in Productivity or Lifestyle, test limiting or shortening trial length.
- Re-evaluate your paywall copy for direct purchase. In Productivity, direct buyers generate $56.95 vs $49.13 for trial users.
- Run cohort analysis on your internal data to identify where your trial users fall on this spectrum.

### #3 LTV
Switzerland, Qatar, and Israel are at the top of the global LTV chart.

**CONTEXT**
Switzerland, Qatar, and Israel lead the global LTV table — yet none of them sit at the center of most app monetization roadmaps. Switzerland LTV $28.46; Qatar $27.46; Israel $27.02. Each market has a distinct profile.

**ACTIONABLE TAKEAWAYS**
- Run price point tests at +15-20% above your current EU and MEA base prices in Switzerland, Qatar, and Israel.
- Break these three markets out of their regional pricing buckets.
- Use Switzerland as your primary European price sensitivity test market.

### #4 Pricing
Median annual prices grew the fastest at +10.3% ($34.83 → $38.42) from 2023 to 2025, followed by monthly (+8.3%) and weekly (+7.0%).

**DATA EVIDENCE**
Weekly $6.99→$7.48 (+7.0%); Monthly $11.99→$12.99 (+8.3%); Annual $34.83→$38.42 (+10.3%). European weekly prices surged 41.7%; LATAM and MEA both grew 24–27%.

**ACTIONABLE TAKEAWAYS**
- If you don't offer a weekly plan, you're leaving your highest-ARPU segment unaddressed. Weekly subscribers pay 10x the annual equivalent.
- Position weekly as the "try before you commit" option, priced at 2–3x the monthly equivalent per unit of time.
- Test weekly pricing in Europe and LATAM first.

### #32 Market – North America
North America is the least concentrated market (92.1%) and the highest-converting, the best region for new entrants.

**DATA EVIDENCE**
Install-to-trial 14.5% (#1). Trial-to-paid 29.2% (#1). Direct install-to-paid 7.38% (#1, nearly 2x Europe). R1 47.5% (#1). R5 21.0% (#1). Revenue concentration 92.1% (least concentrated).

### #33 Pricing – Europe
Europe now charges 29–39% more than North America, and the gap is widening.

**DATA EVIDENCE**
Weekly: Europe $9.38 vs NA $7.29 (+29%). Monthly: $15.25 vs $10.95 (+39%). Annual: $46.98 vs $36.23 (+30%). Monthly gap exploded from 6% (2023) to 39% (2025).

**ACTIONABLE TAKEAWAYS**
- If you're pricing Europe the same as NA, you're likely undercharging.
- Test regional pricing aggressively. 39% monthly gap = 39% revenue uplift per subscriber.
- Pair higher European prices with trials. Europe has best D380 annual retention (21.3%).

### #34 Pricing – MEA

**DATA EVIDENCE**

**ACTIONABLE TAKEAWAYS**

### #35 Conversions – LATAM
