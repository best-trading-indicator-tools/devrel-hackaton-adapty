## 1/

**YOUR GROWTH MAP IS OUTDATED.**

When we compared 2025 revenue to 2024 by country, the growth wasn’t concentrated where many teams usually expect it to be.

Japan leads with 95.6% year-over-year growth. Mexico follows at 88.3%, and Turkey at 72.3%. Brazil crosses 60%, while Australia, Canada, Germany, France, Italy, and the Netherlands all grow in the mid-50% range.

What’s interesting isn’t just the size of these numbers. It’s the distribution. Growth isn’t limited to one dominant Western market. It’s happening across Asia, Latin America, and Europe at the same time.

This doesn’t mean shifting focus away from the US entirely. But it may mean reassessing allocation.

## 2/

**NOT US. NOT UK. NOT GERMANY.**

While our content team is finishing the State of In-App Subscriptions 2026 report, here’s one early insight.

The 3 countries with the highest subscriber LTV aren’t US, Germany, or UK.

They are: Israel, Qatar, and Norway.

Most apps don’t optimise for these markets individually. They’re grouped into EU or MEA tiers, and the premium signal gets averaged out. App makers are suppressing yield without realising it.

Switzerland’s average and median LTV are just 11.2% apart — not whale-driven. Qatar shows strong willingness to pay with a tight spread. Israel has the highest average LTV with a large enough sample to trust.

If you’re testing geo pricing, start with these 3. The data supports 15–20% above your current EU and MEA base prices.

## 3/

**YOUR BILLING STRATEGY IS STUCK IN 2018.**

For the past 3 months, we’ve been deep in the data for our State of in-app subscriptions report — and we’re almost ready to share it.

While we’re finalizing everything, here’s one insight we keep circling back to:

At low prices:

- Monthly LTV is **$13.4**.
- Weekly reaches **$27.7**.
- Annual delivers **$23.5**.

At high pricing:

- Monthly ends at **$54.1**.
- Weekly reaches **$64.8**.
- Annual leads at **$86.4**.

Monthly captures neither the short-term upside of weekly nor the long-term value of annual.

If monthly is leading your paywall, test two things:

- Compare it directly against weekly at lower tiers.
- Compare it against annual at higher tiers — especially if you're optimizing for 1-year LTV.

## 4/

**GROWTH MAY TAKES LONGER THAN YOU THINK.**

People often assume the newest apps are the ones driving the market. But when you break down 2025 revenue by launch year, a different pattern appears.

The largest share of total 2025 revenue comes from apps launched in 2023. Not 2024. Not 2025.

2024 cohorts are growing, but they haven’t overtaken 2023 yet. And 2025 launches are simply too early in their lifecycle to generate a meaningful revenue share. Older apps from 2012–2019 still contribute, but they’re no longer the dominant force.

What this shows is fairly simple: revenue dominance usually happens after launch, not at launch. It takes time to optimize pricing, acquisition, retention, and monetization. The cohort that leads isn’t the newest one — it’s the one that had enough time to scale.

## 5/

**YOU’VE BEEN RUNNING THE WRONG EXPERIMENT ALL THIS TIME.**

We thought about cutting this insight — until we looked at the numbers again: five translations can drive more impact than weeks of pricing optimization.

Most A/B roadmaps are 80% price experiments.

And price tests deliver the lowest LTV uplift we measured:

28.3% CR uplift. 45.5% LTV uplift. Dead last.

Localization — translating your paywall and adjusting currency — delivers 62.3% LTV uplift.

That’s 37% more than repricing.

And it’s not a hard test. No pricing strategist. No month of analysis. Just translated strings and the right markets.

A $9.99 English paywall shown to a user in Japan or Mexico adds friction. Remove it, and conversion moves.

If your next 3 experiments are price tests, replace one with a locale test.

Start with Japan, Mexico, or Turkey.
